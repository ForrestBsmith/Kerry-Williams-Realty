Information About Brokerage Services

Texas law requires real estate license holders to give the following information about brokerage services to prospective buyers, tenants, sellers, and landlords.

Types of Real Estate License Holders

  • A broker is responsible for all brokerage activities, including acts performed by sales agents sponsored by the broker.
  • A sales agent must be sponsored by a broker and works clients on behalf of that broker.

A Broker’s Minimum Duties Required by Law

  • Put the interests of the client above all others, including the broker’s own.
  • Inform the client of any material information about the property or transaction received by the broker.
  • Answer the client’s questions and present any offer or counter offer.
  • Treat all parties to a real estate transaction honestly and fairly.

A License Holder Can Represent

Owner (seller/landlord): The broker becomes the property owner’s agent through an agreement with the owner.

Buyer/tenant: The broker becomes the buyer/tenant’s agent through a written representation agreement.

Both – intermediary: Must obtain written consent and establish broker obligations in the agreement.

To Avoid Disputes

  • Put all agreements between you and a broker in writing and clearly establish duties.
  • State who will pay the broker, when payment is made, and how it will be calculated.
  • Define when the broker is paid during intermediary relationships.

As Agent for Buyer/Tenant

The broker becomes the buyer/tenant’s agent and must inform the buyer of any material information known by the agent, including disclosures from the seller or seller’s agent.

As Agent for Both – Intermediary

  • Must treat all parties to the transaction impartially and fairly.
  • May, with written consent, appoint a different license holder to communicate with and advise each party.
  • Must not disclose confidential information unless authorized in writing.

As Subagent

Acts as a subagent when aiding a buyer without an agreement to represent the buyer. Must place the interests of the owner first.
